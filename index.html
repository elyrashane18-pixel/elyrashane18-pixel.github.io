Why It Matters

Trees do more than
look nice.

Cleaner Air

One mature tree pulls roughly 48 lbs of CO2 out of the air each year. Multiply that by 48,000 trees and you start to see why this work adds up.

Better Water

Roots slow down rainwater before it runs off into drains. They filter it, store it underground, and help keep rivers from flooding neighborhoods downstream.

Wildlife Habitat

A single native oak can support over 500 species of insects, birds, and mammals. When we plant local species, we're not just growing trees. We're rebuilding ecosystems.

Cooler Cities

Cities with good tree cover run up to 10 degrees cooler than those without. That matters a lot if you live in Phoenix, Houston, or anywhere summers keep getting longer.

Our Progress

Four years in.
Still going.

TreeRoots started in 2021 with six people and a pickup truck full of saplings. Today we have over 3,000 volunteers across 62 locations and we're still adding more every month.
